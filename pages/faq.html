Everything you need to know about AGIChainAI, the AGIC token and the technology behind the network.

AGIChainAI is a decentralized infrastructure project that combines blockchain and artificial intelligence. It aims to provide open, verifiable access to AI — including chat, autonomous agents, a developer SDK, a marketplace and a wallet — on a network owned and governed by its community.

Narrow AI performs one specific task, like translating text or recognizing images. AGI — Artificial General Intelligence — refers to systems that can learn, reason and adapt across many different domains, similar to human intelligence. AGIChainAI is building infrastructure designed for that next generation of systems.

Centralized AI concentrates power: one company decides pricing, access and rules, and users cannot verify how the system behaves. Decentralization distributes computation, governance and value across many participants — removing single points of failure and making the system auditable by anyone.

AGIC is the network's utility token. It is used to pay for AI inference, agent execution, API calls and marketplace purchases; to stake as security collateral for validators and compute nodes; and to vote in network governance. Details are on the Tokenomics page.

Total supply is fixed at 1,000,000,000 (1 billion) AGIC. There is no inflation. The allocation is: 40% community & ecosystem, 20% staking rewards, 15% treasury, 15% team (vested with a 12-month cliff and 36-month linear vesting), and 10% liquidity.

Agents are autonomous programs with their own on-chain identity and wallet. They can schedule tasks, analyze data, call APIs, interact with smart contracts and pay for their own compute in AGIC — enabling fully automated, machine-to-machine workflows.
